### Lattice vision clarification (architecture-level decisions)

Extended design conversation that resolved the multi-user model. Key decisions logged:

**Lattice is 1:1 with a user.** There is no shared Lattice and no workspace-with-members model. Each person creates their own Lattice. It is private to them.

**Two classes of people in the system:**
- **Lattice owners** ‚Äî have a Lattice with all domains (only Parcel functional initially). See full domain nav (teasers for Personal etc.). Have their own Parcel(s).
- **Parcel members** ‚Äî have full edit access to someone else's Parcel. Go directly to that Parcel on login. Do not see the owner's Lattice. Can also have their own Lattice independently.

**Roles simplified:**
- `owner` ‚Äî owns the Lattice and the property; full admin rights in Parcel
- `member` ‚Äî Parcel collaborator; full edit rights in Parcel, no Lattice access
- `viewer` role deprecated ‚Äî renamed to `member`

**Invitation flows (two distinct flows):**
1. Invite as Parcel member ‚Äî gives access to a specific property, no Lattice
2. Invite to create own Lattice ‚Äî user creates their account and their own Parcel

### C2 schema proposed (not yet built)

Proposed two tables scoped to `lattice_id`, owner-only RLS:

**`global_context`** ‚Äî one row per Lattice (upserted), JSONB fields:
- `goals` ‚Äî life-level strategic goals (distinct from Parcel's property-level `goals` table)
- `planning_assumptions` ‚Äî freeform key-value (e.g., "stay in house 10+ years")
- `risk_preferences` ‚Äî freeform key-value
- `thresholds` ‚Äî named spending/budget limits

**`global_commitments`** ‚Äî structured rows for major cross-domain financial commitments:
- `name`, `domain` (parcel/personal/null), `amount`, `target_year`, `target_quarter`, `notes`
- Structured (not JSON) so C3 can query and aggregate
